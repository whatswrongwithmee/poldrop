Privacy Policy

This policy explains what information Polydrop collects when you use our airdrop platform, why we collect it, and the choices you have. We keep data collection to the minimum needed to verify eligibility and protect the reward pool from abuse.

Last updated June 9, 2026

1Information we collect

Polydrop is a non-custodial platform. We collect only what is necessary to confirm that you are a genuine, unique participant and to distribute rewards correctly.

  • Wallet address — the public blockchain address you connect, used to check eligibility and send rewards.
  • On-chain activity — publicly available Polymarket trading and market-participation data associated with your address.
  • Technical data — IP address, browser type, and device information, collected automatically to keep the service secure.
  • Local preferences — theme choice and connection state stored in your browser (never sent to us).

2How we use your information

We use the information above strictly to operate the airdrop:

  • Verify that your wallet meets the eligibility requirements.
  • Calculate and distribute your reward allocation.
  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and Sybil activity.
  • Maintain the security, stability, and performance of the platform.

We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising or profiling unrelated to the airdrop.

3What we never collect

We will never ask for your private keys or seed phrase

Connecting a wallet only shares your public address. Anyone asking for your seed phrase or private keys is attempting to scam you — that is never us.

We also do not collect names, email addresses, government IDs, or payment-card details. Participation is pseudonymous and tied only to your public wallet address.

4Anti-Sybil monitoring

To keep the reward pool fair for real users, we analyze on-chain patterns and technical signals to detect Sybil behavior — a single person creating many wallets or accounts to farm rewards.

This may include clustering related addresses, reviewing funding sources, and flagging coordinated activity. Wallets identified as Sybil may have their eligibility revoked. We use this analysis only for fraud prevention, not to identify you personally.

5Sharing & third parties

We share data only when necessary to run the service:

  • Blockchain analytics providers — to support eligibility checks and Sybil detection.
  • Infrastructure providers — hosting and security services that process technical data on our behalf.
  • Legal requirements — if required by applicable law or a valid legal request.

The blockchain itself is public: any transaction we send is permanently visible on-chain. We never sell personal data to third parties.

6Cookies & local storage

We use your browser's local storage to remember your theme preference and whether your wallet is connected. These stay on your device and are not used for cross-site tracking or advertising. You can clear them at any time from your browser settings.

7Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of the personal data we hold about you, and to object to certain processing. Because eligibility is tied to a public wallet address, some on-chain data cannot be deleted, as it lives permanently on the blockchain.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below.

8Data retention

We keep eligibility and fraud-prevention data only for as long as needed to run the airdrop and meet legal obligations. Technical logs are retained for a limited period and then deleted or anonymized.

9Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date above. Significant changes will be highlighted on this page. Continued use of Polydrop after an update means you accept the revised policy.
